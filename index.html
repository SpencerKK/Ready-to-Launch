Q & A

Why start a podcast?

Honey, where do I start? At the end of the day, everyone has their own personal reason for launching a podcast. What applies to everyone is the future of the podcast industry. Where an estimated only 20% of Americans knew what a podcast was in 2006, today that number is 70%. I interviewed the founder of the Forbes Podcast on my own show, The Ready to Launch Podcast, who said anyone that starts a podcast today and sticks with it will benefit enormously in a few years from now when podcasting consumption and production is exponentially more popular. If you have a reason for wanting to start your show, but are unsure of whether the timing is right, THE TIME IS RIGHT.

Can I make money from podcasting?

YES! In the past five years, ad revenue from podcasting has increased 1000%. In 2019, podcast ad revenue was an estimated $514 million, an estimated $659 million in 2020, and in 2021, ad revenue is expected to surpass $1 billion. You do not need a massive audience to find sponsors that are excited to work with you. You need a dedicated, niche audience that trusts your word and that you can build a relationship with. Podcasts with only a few hundred downloads per episode can still make thousands of dollars per episode if they establish their niche correctly and build a relationship with an enthusiastic and satisfied sponsor. We will work toward this together!

How do I create relationships with high-quality guests?

Booking guests is one of the most exciting parts of podcasting because if you are confident in your approach, you have the ability to build relationships with some of your greatest role models. Still, to a lot of people, this aspect of podcasting can be intimidating and seem more like a time commitment and a chore with little return. Together, we will establish a method for reaching out to your dream guests that you can be proud of. Through my own podcast, I have had the chance to speak to people I never would have dreamed of speaking to and it all comes down to your ability to communicate your podcast’s legitimacy and your authentic desire to speak with them.

How much of a time commitment is podcasting?

Time does not equal return. Once we establish your podcasting goals and approach to launch and growth, you should not feel overwhelmed with the amount of time your podcast is requiring of you. A huge part of this is because when you podcast with Ready to Launch Podcasts it is no longer a one-man show. You have the guidance, the feedback, and the Ready to Launch Podcasts network available to you. A lack of collaboration is what holds so many podcasts back and ends up costing podcasters so much time. Our consultations will as well accelerate your learning curve and encourage the consistent development of your show.

Do I need to buy equipment?

This is up to you, but if you are in podcasting for the long haul, I highly recommend you do. Yes, our phones provide pretty good microphones, but you can always tell the difference when a podcaster records with a proper microphone vs. when they record with their phone. I still use the same microphone I bought when I first started out, and it has been one of my greatest investments. When I first started The Ready to Launch Podcast, my friends all told me they expected it to have low-quality audio (thanks!) but were so pleasantly surprised that it sounded so clear and crisp. Not only did this make me take pride in my work, but when guests are deciding whether they want to come on your show or not, oftentimes it depends on the quality of your production. My microphone cost me around $100 and is one of the best on the market!

Why work with Ready to Launch Podcasts?

You need to choose where you are going to invest in your show—while it’s true that there are an estimated 750,000 launched podcasts, a staggering number of those go out of production by episode #7. This phenomenon has been coined the notorious Podfade and often this can be seen on platforms like Anchor. I believe this is because podcasting is still new; there is still an inherent lack of community between podcasts, a lack of knowledge around how to have a successful launch, and as time goes on with little return, people forget their WHY instead of honing in on their niche. I get that it could be isolating and discouraging, and this could lead people to only publish a few episodes, but this doesn’t have to be the case! It is important to me that any podcaster working with Ready to Launch Podcasts actually experiences podcasting how it should be experienced: with a return on investment, a growing network of other podcasters to cross-collaborate with, with quality guests that they can be enthusiastic to speak to, the confidence in their ability to land a dream sponsor that aligns with the vision of their show and, most importantly, to actually feel liberated throughout this process and to take pride in their show. THAT IS HOW IT SHOULD ALWAYS BE! Start podcasting with Ready to Launch Podcasts knowing that you’re doing so strategically and that you’re a part of a network that reflects the caliber of your show.
